RTL-to-GDSII / ASIC Flow GitHub ↗

4-Stage Pipelined RV32I Core — Signed-Off to GDSII at 333MHz

Designed a 4-stage pipelined RV32I core (IF→EX→MEM→WB) with hazard forwarding, load-use stalling, and branch handling, then carried it through a complete open-source ASIC flow — Yosys synthesis, OpenROAD place-and-route, Sky130 PDK — to a signed-off, manufacturable layout. Most student RTL projects stop at simulation; this one closes the loop between "the RTL is correct" and "the RTL becomes a chip."

  • Real optimization story, not just one number: baseline closed clean at 100MHz, then pushed to a 333MHz optimized target. A stretch attempt at 500MHz was tried and honestly documented as a failed timing closure — a real data point on where this design's timing wall sits, not a number quietly discarded.
  • Verified bit-exact against a from-scratch RV32I golden model before synthesis; every hazard path (ALU forwarding, load-use stall, taken/not-taken branch, JAL) exercised in a self-checking testbench.
